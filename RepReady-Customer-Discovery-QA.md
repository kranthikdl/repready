# RepReady — Customer Discovery & Customization Q&A

Use this questionnaire in your first working session with a new customer. Answers feed directly into the Coaching Profile configuration and any planned platform extensions.

---

## Section 1 — Sales Methodology & Philosophy

**1.1** What sales methodology does your team follow?
*(e.g., MEDDIC, MEDDPICC, Challenger, SPIN, Sandler, Command of the Message, your own framework)*

**1.2** How strictly is it enforced? Is it a rigid checklist, a set of guiding principles, or somewhere in between?

**1.3** Are there specific stages, questions, or phrases that every rep must hit on every call? If yes, list them.

**1.4** What does "a great discovery call" look like to you? Describe it in concrete behaviors, not outcomes.

**1.5** What is the single most common coaching note you give to reps after listening to a call?

---

## Section 2 — Team Structure

**2.1** How many SDRs / AEs will use RepReady?

**2.2** How many managers or coaches will review sessions and scorecards?

**2.3** Do different rep segments need different coaching profiles?
*(e.g., enterprise vs. mid-market reps, new hire vs. tenured, inbound vs. outbound)*

**2.4** Who owns configuration — the enablement team, frontline manager, or RevOps?

**2.5** Will reps self-select sessions, or will a manager assign them?

---

## Section 3 — Call Types & Focus Areas

**3.1** Which call types are in scope for coaching?
*(Cold outbound, warm inbound, discovery, demo, follow-up, negotiation, renewal — rank by priority)*

**3.2** For each call type, what is the #1 behavior you want the AI to catch and coach in real time?

**3.3** Are there call types that should be excluded from scoring entirely?

**3.4** What is your average call duration for each type? *(Relevant for scoring threshold calibration)*

**3.5** Do calls happen primarily via phone, video (Zoom/Teams), or both?

---

## Section 4 — Scorecard Dimensions & Success Metrics

**4.1** If you were grading a rep's call on four dimensions today, what would they be?
*(The platform defaults to Discovery, Objection Handling, Next-Step Discipline, and Overall Readiness — do these fit, or would you rename / replace any?)*

**4.2** For each dimension, what does a score of 90 look like vs. a score of 50?

**4.3** Are any dimensions weighted more heavily than others in your evaluation culture?

**4.4** Is there a minimum acceptable score per dimension before a rep is considered "call-ready"?

**4.5** How do you currently measure rep readiness — call shadowing, role-plays, manager review, or something else?

---

## Section 5 — Real-Time Coaching Triggers

**5.1** List the exact phrases or patterns that signal a rep is losing the call.
*(e.g., "let me think about it," "send me some information," long silence after a question)*

**5.2** List competitor names or product names that should always trigger an immediate high-priority coaching cue.

**5.3** Are there phrases your reps overuse that should trigger a coaching nudge?
*(e.g., "absolutely," "does that make sense," filler words)*

**5.4** What objections come up most often, and do you have a preferred response framework for each?

**5.5** Are there any topics that should never be discussed on a call without manager escalation?
*(e.g., pricing below a floor, specific SLA commitments)*

---

## Section 6 — Simulation Scripts & Practice Scenarios

**6.1** Do you have existing call recordings or transcripts you use for training today?

**6.2** What are the top 3 scenarios every new rep must be able to handle before going live?

**6.3** For each scenario, describe: the prospect persona, their primary objection, and the ideal rep response.

**6.4** Should simulation scripts reflect specific verticals or company sizes?
*(e.g., a CFO at a 500-person SaaS company vs. a VP of Sales at a startup)*

**6.5** How often do you want simulation content refreshed — quarterly, after product launches, after win/loss analysis?

---

## Section 7 — Integrations & Data Flow

**7.1** What CRM do you use? *(Salesforce, HubSpot, other)*
Is call activity logged automatically today, or manually?

**7.2** What conversation intelligence tools are in your stack, if any?
*(Gong, Chorus, Salesloft, Outreach, etc.)*

**7.3** Do you use Microsoft Teams for internal or external calls?

**7.4** Where do post-call summaries and scorecards need to land?
*(CRM record, Slack channel, email, manager dashboard)*

**7.5** Are there compliance or data residency requirements that affect where call audio or transcripts can be stored or processed?

---

## Section 8 — Reporting & Manager Workflow

**8.1** What does the weekly or monthly rep review process look like today?

**8.2** What reports or views does a frontline manager need at a glance?
*(e.g., average readiness score by rep, coaching prompt frequency, trend over time)*

**8.3** Should managers be able to add their own written feedback on top of AI-generated scorecards?

**8.4** How do you handle reps who score consistently low — is there a formal PIP process RepReady should support?

**8.5** Who receives the post-call summary — the rep, the manager, or both?

---

## Section 9 — Rollout & Change Management

**9.1** Have your reps used AI coaching or call recording tools before? What was the adoption experience?

**9.2** Is there union, works council, or HR policy that governs monitoring of sales calls?

**9.3** What is your target go-live date, and what does a successful pilot look like after 30 days?

**9.4** Who is the internal champion driving this initiative?

**9.5** What would cause you to consider this a failed implementation after 90 days?

---

*Completed answers feed into: Coaching Profile (Methodology, Triggers, Scorecard tabs), simulation script library, integration configuration, and any planned dimension extensions.*
